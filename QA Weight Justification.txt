QA SCORING WEIGHT JUSTIFICATION
=================================


RESOLUTION / PROGRESS TOWARD RESOLUTION — 30%

The highest weight because it is the entire point of a support interaction. Every other
category exists in service of this one. An agent can communicate beautifully, document
thoroughly, and escalate correctly — but if the customer leaves without progress toward
a solution, the interaction failed at its core purpose. The 30% weight signals that
outcomes, not process compliance, define quality.


TECHNICAL ACCURACY — 20%

The second-highest weight because resolution is impossible without it. Correct diagnosis,
logical troubleshooting, and accurate technical guidance are the tools that make resolution
achievable. Poor technical accuracy doesn't just fail the customer in the moment — it often
compounds the problem and extends the case life. It outweighs communication and ownership
because a technically sound interaction that is imperfectly communicated can still resolve
an issue; the reverse is rarely true.


COMMUNICATION & CALL CONTROL — 15%

Tied with Ownership because communication is the medium through which all support is
delivered. Even technically correct guidance fails if the customer cannot understand or
follow it. This weight reflects that how an agent manages the interaction — setting
expectations, controlling flow, adapting to the customer's level — directly affects whether
good technical work translates into a good outcome. It does not outweigh technical accuracy
because communication skill cannot substitute for correct diagnosis.


CUSTOMER OWNERSHIP — 15%

Tied with Communication because ownership is what separates agents who resolve cases from
agents who process tickets. Follow-through on commitments, disconnect follow-up, and
accountability for the customer's outcome are what prevent cases from falling through the
cracks between touches. The equal weight with Communication reflects that ownership failures
(missed callbacks, premature closure, no follow-up) are just as damaging to the customer
experience as poor communication — but neither defines quality on its own.


ESCALATION JUDGMENT — 10%

Lower than the top four because escalation is situational — most interactions do not require
it. However, when it is relevant, poor judgment in either direction (escalating prematurely
to avoid work, or failing to escalate a legitimate management or legal-risk request) can have
outsized consequences. The 10% weight ensures it is taken seriously without penalizing agents
whose cases never warranted escalation.


CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE — 5%

Weighted lowest alongside Documentation because it is largely a derivative outcome of the
four categories above. When an agent resolves the issue accurately, communicates clearly, and
owns the interaction, a good customer experience typically follows. The 5% weight acknowledges
that professionalism, empathy, and effort-reduction matter — especially for vulnerable
customers — but ensures QA does not become a personality contest or mistake a pleasant-but-
ineffective interaction as high quality.


DOCUMENTATION & NOTES — 5%

Equal to Customer Experience because documentation is a continuity tool, not a quality signal
in itself. Good notes support the next agent and protect the customer's history — but thorough
notes do not compensate for poor troubleshooting or missed ownership. The 5% weight reflects
the principle that documentation should not outweigh actual support quality. It is scored to
drive the right behavior without inflating scores for agents who write well but help poorly.


---

SUMMARY

The weights descend in proportion to how directly each category drives customer resolution.
Resolution first, the skills that enable it next (Technical Accuracy, Communication,
Ownership), then the situational safeguards (Escalation Judgment), then the experiential
and administrative layers that matter but do not define the outcome on their own.

  Resolution / Progress Toward Resolution    30%
  Technical Accuracy                         20%
  Communication & Call Control               15%
  Customer Ownership                         15%
  Escalation Judgment                        10%
  Customer Experience                         5%
  Documentation & Notes                       5%
                                           -----
  Total                                     100%
